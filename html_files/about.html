The Story of Joe Andrea Illam

Our Roots & Inspiration

The inspiration began with Mr. Joe Britto (elder brother of founder Mr. Arokya Selvaraj), who served as the Dean of Loyola College. He dedicated his life to supporting underprivileged tribal, SC, and ST students. After his tragic passing in an accident, his vision became the seed that grew into the Illam.

Mr. Arokya Selvaraj, working as a Correspondent in a government school, noticed many children attended school simply because it provided food, lacking safety at home. This motivated him to establish a secure sanctuary offering free shelter, nutritious food, and quality education.

In the early stages, Mr. Andrea, a dedicated supporter from the United States, played a massive role by raising crucial foundational funds. Honoring this collaborative legacy, Joe Andrea Illam was born.

A New Generation of Service

Following her father's retirement, Ms. Amala Sherline left a secure, well-paying position at Aavin Milk to find true personal fulfillment in serving society. As the current Correspondent, she successfully manages the trust and school today.

Students who join initially face deep confidence challenges, but blossom into successful individuals. Today, many thriving alumni return to actively give back—donating funds, groceries, and resources to support the next generation.
